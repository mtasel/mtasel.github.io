Māori Creation: Rangi and Papa

This is a pastel painting representing my interpretation of the Māori creation myth, Rangi and Papa. Papa, Earth Mother, lays below to form the earth while Rangi, Sky Father, lays above to form the sky. Tane, god of forests, stands between them, pushing them apart to allow light and warmth to enter. Rangi's tears fall from the sky as rain and cause the great flood (represented in the Earth Mother's hair) and Tāwhiri, god of wind, sends strong winds out of anger towards Tane.
